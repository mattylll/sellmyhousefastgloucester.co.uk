That buyer's purchaser may also need to sell, and so on. In Gloucester, chains of four or five linked transactions are common.</p><p>The most common causes of chain collapse include:</p><ul><li><strong>Mortgage problems:</strong> A buyer's mortgage application is declined or down-valued, a particular risk in Gloucester where buyers at the lower price point are financially stretched</li><li><strong>Survey issues:</strong> A survey on any property in the chain reveals problems that cause the buyer to renegotiate or withdraw, common with Gloucester's Victorian and post-war stock</li><li><strong>Change of circumstances:</strong> A buyer loses their job, faces a relationship breakdown, or changes their mind</li><li><strong>Construction concerns:</strong> In Gloucester specifically, the discovery of non-standard construction in a post-war property can cause an immediate mortgage refusal and chain collapse</li><li><strong>Conveyancing delays:</strong> Slow legal work, missing documents, or complex title issues delay the chain beyond other parties' tolerance</li></ul><h2>Prevention Strategies</h2><h3>Get a Pre-Sale Survey</h3><p>Commissioning a homebuyer's report or building survey on your own property before marketing gives you advance warning of issues.
